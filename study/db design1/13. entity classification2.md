* entity classification2

* strong entity and weak entity
1. strong entity
it can exist independently. it is not depend on other entities
  - example: employee, user, department
2. weak entity
it depends on a strong entity
  - must have a strong entity's pk
  - example: dependent depend on specific employee

* special entity for structural relationship
1. associative entity
it is derived between two or more entities. relationship has property and should be dealt with in business
  - conceptual essence: for saving property depending on relationship. 
    - for example, score or taking a class occurs between student and subject. it is not a property of student or subject. 
    - structural meaning: resolve M:N relationship
2. supertype/subtype entity
it is similar to the inheritance concept
  - supertype: superior entity that has a common property of subtype entity
  - subtype: sub entity
  - example: employee is supertype, full-time employee and contract employee are subtype
